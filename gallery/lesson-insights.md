## Lesson: Share the Cookies

### What this lesson teaches
Fractions arise naturally from fair sharing. When N cookies can't divide evenly among P people, you need to cut the remainder into pieces — introducing halves, quarters, and so on.

### The core mechanic (the math to do FIRST)
Before touching anything:
1. Count the cookies (N) and the people (P)
2. Divide: N ÷ P = how much each person gets
3. If N ≥ P: give each person ⌊N/P⌋ whole cookies, then slice the remainder into P equal pieces
4. If N < P: slice all cookies into P pieces, give one slice to each person

**Examples:**
- 4 cookies ÷ 2 people = 2 each → just share 2 whole to each
- 5 cookies ÷ 2 people = 2½ each → share 2 whole to each, then slice 1 cookie in half, give 1 half to each
- 5 cookies ÷ 4 people = 1¼ each → share 1 whole to each, slice the last cookie into 4 quarters, give 1 quarter to each

### The two interaction modes
The top toolbar has two buttons:
- **Hand (🤚) = Sharing mode**: click a cookie piece in the pool to select it, then click a character to give it to them
- **Knife (🔪) = Slicing mode**: click on a cookie to cut it into pieces

**Critical rule:** You must be in the right mode before interacting. Clicking a cookie in share mode does nothing to it; you need knife mode to cut it.

### How slicing works (technical detail for agents)
The slice interaction is NOT a standard button click. The cookie element is rendered as a `button[disabled]` in the accessibility tree, which means `playwright-cli click` and normal `mousedown/mouseup` sequences won't trigger it.

**What does work:** The game's click handler lives on a parent `DIV` with class `relative flex w-full max-w-4xl`. Dispatch a `MouseEvent('click')` to the element at z-stack index 3 (using `document.elementsFromPoint`) at the cookie's center coordinates:

```js
// In eval:
var cx = 790, cy = 182;  // cookie center — find via getBoundingClientRect()
var els = document.elementsFromPoint(cx, cy);
for (var i = 0; i < els.length; i++) {
  var fk = Object.keys(els[i]).find(k => k.startsWith('__reactFiber'));
  if (fk && els[i][fk].memoizedProps?.onClick) {
    els[i].dispatchEvent(new MouseEvent('click', {bubbles:true, cancelable:true, clientX:cx, clientY:cy}));
    break;
  }
}
```

**One click = one slice** (the cookie cuts into the correct number of pieces in one action).

**To find the cookie's center:** scan all DOM elements for the largest element (by area) in the pool area (x > 300, y between 50–420, width 60–200px). Its center is your click target.

### Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | What happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Clicking cookie in share mode | Nothing happens | Switch to knife mode first (click 🔪) |
| Using `mousemove`+`mousedown`+`mouseup` CLI commands | Cookie doesn't register the slice | Use JS `eval` dispatch (see above) |
| Clicking multiple times | Creates extra pieces, corrupts state | ONE click per cookie = 4 pieces (for quarters) |
| Using old refs after mode switch | "Ref not found" errors | Always re-`snapshot` after switching modes |
| Assigning pieces without checking sizes | Characters get wrong amounts | Check pool piece count matches number of people before distributing |

### Step-by-step guide for 5 cookies ÷ 4 people (1¼ each)

1. **[Share mode]** Click cookie 1 → click Character 1
2. **[Share mode]** Click cookie 2 → click Character 2
3. **[Share mode]** Click cookie 3 → click Character 3
4. **[Share mode]** Click cookie 4 → click Character 4
5. Switch to **knife mode** (click 🔪 button)
6. **[Knife mode]** JS dispatch click at cookie center → 4 quarter pieces appear
7. Switch back to **share mode** (click 🤚 button)
8. **[Share mode]** Re-snapshot to get fresh piece refs
9. **[Share mode]** Click quarter 1 → click Character 1
10. **[Share mode]** Click quarter 2 → click Character 2
11. **[Share mode]** Click quarter 3 → click Character 3
12. **[Share mode]** Click quarter 4 → click Character 4
13. Game advances with "Each person has 1¼ cookies" feedback

### Improvement suggestions for the game

1. **Make the slice interaction more accessible** — the disabled-button pattern means screen readers and automation tools cannot detect or trigger the cookie. A clickable `role="button"` with a proper label ("Slice this cookie") would fix both accessibility and automation reliability.

2. **Add clearer mode feedback** — the toolbar icons (hand vs knife) are small. A text label like "SHARING" / "SLICING" in the toolbar would help students who are confused about which mode they're in.

3. **Prevent state corruption** — if a student repeatedly clicks the same cookie in knife mode, multiple small pieces accumulate. The game should either (a) limit to one slice per cookie, or (b) "un-slice" if clicked again.

4. **Show the math alongside the game** — displaying "5 ÷ 4 = ?" or "each person should get ___ cookies" before the interaction would help students who don't yet know to think fraction-first before touching anything.

5. **Hint button is unclear** — the 🖐️ hint button resets the puzzle entirely rather than giving a nudge. Students who just want a small hint lose their progress. A "nudge" vs "show me" two-level hint would be more useful.
